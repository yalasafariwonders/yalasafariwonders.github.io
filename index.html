FAQs

What is the entrance fee for Yala National Park?
The entrance fee for Yala National Park varies based on the type of visitor. As of 2024 Apr., the fees are as follows:
Foreign Visitors:
1 Aadult: 13100 rupees
2 Aadults: 22000 rupees
3 Adults: 31000 rupees
4 Aadults: 40000 rupees
Entrance fees, subject to minor fluctuations based on currency rates, are payable directly at the park entrance when you begin your safari.
Are safaris available throughout the year?
Yes, Yala Safaris operate year-round. The park's landscape changes with the seasons, offering unique experiences, from lush greenery to dry expanses. Be sure to check the weather conditions for the best possible adventure.
What should I wear on a Safari?
You should wear comfortable clothing that is suitable for the weather. It is also a good idea to wear a hat and sunscreen, as well as insect repellent.
What should I bring for a Yala Safari?
Binoculars (provided), Camera (optional), Sunglasses, Hat, Face mask, Sunscreen, Snacks and drinks, Breakfast (optional), Water
Feel free to bring these optional items to enhance your safari experience.
Is Yala suitable for all ages?
Yes, Yala Safari is suitable for all ages. However, it's important to note that some parts of the park may have rough terrain. Families with young children or individuals with mobility concerns should inform us in advance for appropriate arrangements
Is there a pick-up and drop-off service for the Yala Safari?
Yes, we offer free complimentary pick-up and drop-off service for visitors in the Palatupana, Kirinda, and Tissamaharama areas.
